# Five-Minute Demo Script

Audience: Medicare agency owner.

## 0:00 - Open

Start at `/`.

Say: "This is not a sales bot. It is an internal compliance operations workspace for a Medicare agency. The system reviews conversations, tracks consent, routes work, and preserves auditability. It never recommends Medicare plans or annuities."

Click **Review flagged call**.

## 0:45 - Conversation Review

On the conversation detail page, point out:

- Conversation context
- Review posture
- AI-style summary
- Compliance flags
- Transcript highlights

Paste or use the stored transcript and click **Run AI review**.

Say: "The AI is not choosing a product. It is finding review issues, citing transcript text, and giving the human reviewer a reason."

Confirm one flag with a short reason.

## 2:00 - Consent Boundary

Open `/consents`.

Show Medicare consent records separately from separate retirement-income follow-up consent.

Say: "Consent is append-only. Revocation creates a new record; it does not rewrite history."

## 2:45 - Separate Retirement-Income Workflow

Open `/opportunities`.

Show that retirement-income follow-up has its own queue, assignment rules, consent status, and audit trail.

Say: "If retirement-income interest appears during a Medicare conversation, it does not become a Medicare sales workflow. A human explicitly creates or advances a separate consented workflow."

## 3:30 - Compliance Dashboard

Open `/compliance`.

Filter open or high-severity flags.

Say: "This is the manager and compliance reviewer view: what needs review, who owns it, why it was flagged, and what action was taken."

## 4:15 - Client and Task Traceability

Open `/clients`, then a client detail page, then `/tasks`.

Show timeline, linked tasks, consent state, conversations, flags, and separate follow-up indicators.

Say: "The owner can reconstruct who did what, when, from what source, with what consent status, and what the next action was."

## Close

Say: "The MVP story is one agency, one flagged conversation, one consent boundary, one separate workflow, and a full audit trail. That is the product wedge."
